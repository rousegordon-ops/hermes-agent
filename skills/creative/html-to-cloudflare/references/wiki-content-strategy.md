# Wiki Content Incorporation — Lessons Learned

## What to Add (Personally Significant)

- Interests, likes/dislikes (e.g. hiking, fishing, style, travel preferences)
- Career/project details that reveal something about direction or values
- Health or behavioral patterns
- Financial life decisions
- Travel that reveals preferences or habits

## What to Skip (Trivial/Ephemeral)

- One-off factoids or generic advice answered quickly
- Things that would be outdated in 6 months
- Trivia questions with no broader context

## Content Sensitivity

Gordon corrects overstatements. The rule: **when in doubt, understate rather than overstate significance.**

## Category Conventions

- **Day Hikes** (`hobbies/hiking.md`) — separate from overnight trips. Local Bay Area hikes, park hours, logistics. Tinker Knob training belongs in Backpacking, not here.
- **Backpacking** (`hobbies/backpacking.md`) — overnight wilderness trips. Contains Tinker Knob objective, training hikes, fishing+camping combo, camp requirements.
- **Fishing** (`hobbies/backcountry-fishing.md`) — specifically Sierra backcountry trout fishing. Plunge pools, remote creeks, 2–3 night base camps. This is a component of backpacking.
- **Fitness** (`hobbies/fitness.md`) — strength training, mobility protocol. Includes alcohol use/health habits inline (don't create a separate "Health" page).
- **Style** (`hobbies/personal-style.md`) — clothing fit challenges, brands that work.
- **London trip** (`hobbies/london-trip.md`) — specific trip details, not generic travel advice.

These are three distinct categories. Don't conflate them — a day hike is not a backpacking trip.
